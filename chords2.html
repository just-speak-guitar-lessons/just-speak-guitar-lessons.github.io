The Power Chord

The Power chord is only played on the bass strings so it adds more depth, or power, to songs. A lot of songs that are played with distortion tend to have them in or were an added effect is required on top of the harmonics with the added strength and veracity of the bass notes.
It was invented by an American during the 1900's, he wasn't aiming for these effects he was playing them because he was born with only two fingers on each hand.
For all voice commands for this website, just say, "What to say" at any time to open the Help Box.

The F Power Chord.

This type of Power chord is played the most through out any music that has a Power Chord. If you did see that it's very similar to an F-Barre Chord explained earlier, then you're correct - it is!
In the Chord Diagram, the circles signify where to place your finger tips, note that there are two lower circles together and a top level one also. The note played on the first fret is called the root note simply because its the first bass note. In all, this is the F (root note) Power Chord.

The B Flat Power Chord

This Power chord is played on the B flat root note (1st fret and 2nd string) hence the name. It has exactly the same shape as the previous power chord, except each finger stays on the same fret but moves down just one string each.
If you are learning for the first time, Power chords are without any reasonable doubt the best chord to start with.
It's called a Power Chord because of it's drive and the added power a learner would get from starting to learn with this type of chord and the added power a learner would get from starting to learn with this type of chord.

A power chord is, in fact, a diad which is 2 notes played together. Guitar power chords are very versatile, since the shape can be moved all over the fretboard, as with bar chords. Power chords are mostly played on electric guitars under distortion.
