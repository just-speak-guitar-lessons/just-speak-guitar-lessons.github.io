To start with ...

The rhythm guitar is as popular amongst learners as wanting to learn the lead guitar and their favorite solo. However, it would be impossible to start learning the lead guitar without first learning how to play rhythm guitar. By learning a songs strong, regular repeated pattern of chord changes and timing you will train your sound and sight sensor's in a way that can never be taught in a class room.

Learning a songs rhythm section, will teach you when to start playing a solo rather than jumping in, hoping to catch the right time and ultimately embarrassing you and your band in the process.

How To Keep To A Beat

Strumming a guitar works with the timing. Timing in musical terms means keeping to the beat of a song. Most songs played on a guitar are in 4 /4 timing which in general means there are 4 beats over 4 seconds or play a beat every second ON the second.

This is much easier said that done if you're just starting to learn. To simplify this, a good technique would be to strum downwards one time every second. That's it, you are playing in 4/4 timing, well done! Now using this technique whilst changing chords will not be easy so take your time, keep strumming and try to relax.

A simple way to keep to a beat without counting out loud, is to silently count from 1 to 4, on each second just tap your foot. Each time you tap your foot on the floor just make a strum on your guitar.
